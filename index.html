The Picaresque

The classic Picaresque story has a trickster protagonist, or a Picarro. When Lazarillo de Tormes was first written, it gained popularity because of how different it was from the rest of the Picaresque genre. While Don Quixote and the other stories of knights from this time period were romantic stories of chivalry, Lazarillo was about much more real problems, such as hunger, poverty, and a boy’s simple struggle to survive. It is interesting to note that Lazarillo has a possible kinship with the Maqamat. Additionally, both Lazarillo and Don Quixote provide distinct social commentaries on the Christian society.
